Strategic Analysis

Where We're Strong, Where We're Short, Where to Move

Strengths

  • Computer Science (CHS) — Deep 4-year sequence with 2 AP courses. Already a de facto hub.
  • Pre-Engineering (CVHS) — Unique IDEA/STEAM/DREAM courses have no CHS equivalent. Clear CVHS strength.
  • Manufacturing (CHS) — 7-course pathway through capstone. Strong hands-on infrastructure (bond-funded).
  • Digital Arts (CHS) + Film (CVHS) — Complementary specializations across campuses.
  • Design & Applied Arts (CVHS) — Unique ceramics/glass/jewelry CTE with LBCC articulation.

Shortfalls

  • Zero hard CTE dual credits — 40 articulations, all academic. Massive missed opportunity.
  • Health Sciences lacks depth — No CNA certification (Albany has this). Only basic First Aid/CPR for College Now.
  • No Agriculture/FFA — Despite proximity to OSU's ag programs.
  • Automotive is shallow — 2 courses at each campus. Neither articulates with LBCC's full Auto Tech AAS.
  • CTE pathway naming inconsistency — District website vs. school catalogs don't match.
  • No dedicated CTE center — Salem-Keizer (CTEC) and Eugene (CALCI) both have one.

Where to Move

  • LBCC CTE articulations — Priority: Welding/Fabrication, Machine Tool, Automotive Tech, Culinary, Health Sciences (CNA, Medical Assistant).
  • Health Sciences expansion — Add CNA track. Healthcare = 16% local employment, #1 growth statewide (+40,400 jobs by 2034).
  • Consolidate Automotive — One campus hub instead of shallow sequence at both.
  • Unify Metals/Woodworking naming — Same skills, different labels across campuses.
  • CS College Now — 5-course pathway with zero articulation is the lowest-hanging fruit.

Admin Directive

Alignment Parameters

Non-negotiable constraints from district administration. Every recommendation must satisfy these.

Aligned Grade-Level Courses

Core and elective/CTE courses aligned across campuses at every grade level.

Aligned Course Sequences

Coherent progressions. Capstone courses at end of a sequence may only be at one site.

Yearlong + Semester Mix

Except in core, provide both. Semester courses serve as exploratory on-ramps.

Full Standards Coverage

Sequences must cover all grade-level skills standards in the content area.

Singleton Reduction

Eliminate one-off courses that don't connect to a pathway.

Overall Course Reduction

Fewer, stronger pathways. Every remaining course earns its place with data.

Increase Advanced Opportunities

Expand honors, AP, and dual credit (College Now) — especially in CTE.

Support & Scaffolded Classes

Ensure scaffolding for students who need it. Acceleration for those ready.

Collaboration Protocol

Combined Team Time

Structured cross-campus collaboration. Faculty from both schools work together with data.

  • Macdonald is a district-wide single point of failure for CS — if he leaves, the entire 5-course pathway across both campuses collapses.
  • Keim carries 9+ CTE preps across 2 pathways (Manufacturing + Automotive). Alignment could reduce prep burden by consolidating Automotive to one campus.
